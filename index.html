The trouble with Bears

By Evan Wild

Tall, lumbering, angry, dangerous... proud, independent creatures...

So what are bears really like, and why does the media portray them this way?

Types of bear

Bears come in two varieties — large and medium. No small bears unless they're cubs.

They adapt to urban or forest life. Table below shows bear facts:

Useful facts about different types of bears
Bear Type Coat Adult size Habitat Lifespan Diet
Wild Brown or black 1.4 to 2.8 meters Woods and forests 25 to 28 years Fish, meat, plants
Urban North Face 18 to 22 Condos and coffee shops 20 to 32 years Starbucks, sushi

Habitats and Eating habits

Wild bears hunt in forests/rivers. Urban bears scavenge in cities.

Mating rituals

Bears decorate to attract mates — reeds/cave art or mood lighting/music.
